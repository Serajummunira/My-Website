2. Brief Format Use Case:

Use Case Name: Drive/Fly the Flying Car

Primary Actor: Player

Goal in Context: The player drives/fly a flying car in an open-world environment, completing missions and avoiding obstacles.

Preconditions: The player must have launched the game.

Postconditions: The game state is saved after the player completes a mission or crashes.

Main Flow: The player selects a mission, navigates the flying car, and completes objectives (e.g., racing, delivering packages, or battling enemies).

3. Fowler Format Use Case:

Use Case Name: Drive/Fly the Flying Car

Goal: Enable the player to drive or fly a flying car through a dynamic environment.

Actors: Player (primary), AI-driven car (secondary, for challenges or missions)

Scope: Entire game environment including cities, obstacles, missions, and opponents.

Level: User goal

Primary Scenario: 1. Player starts the game. 2. The player selects a mission from the available options. 3. The player drives or flies the car through the mission, avoiding obstacles. 4. The player completes the mission objectives or fails based on the time or obstacles. 5. The game state is saved, and the player can continue to the next mission or replay the current one.

Alternative Scenarios: 1. If the player crashes, the game prompts the player to retry or exit. 2. If the player selects a different vehicle or upgrade, the game allows switching without resetting progress.

Special Requirements: Realistic driving physics, seamless integration of flying mechanic, user-friendly controls.

3. Fully Dressed Use Case:

Use Case Name: Drive/Fly the Flying Car

Goal in Context: The player aims to successfully navigate a flying car through various challenges, including both flying and driving tasks.

Actors: Player (primary actor), AI-driven car (secondary actor)

Stakeholders and Interests: - Player: Wants a fun, challenging, and immersive experience. - Game Developers: Want the game to be engaging and perform well on different devices.

Preconditions: - The player has launched the game and is ready to select a mission. - The player’s controls are functioning properly.

Postconditions: - If the mission is completed successfully, the player's score is updated. - If the mission is failed, the player has the option to restart or quit.

Main Flow: 1. The player launches the game and selects the "Start" option. 2. The player chooses a mission, which could involve various objectives (e.g., racing or completing delivery). 3. The player navigates the flying car through the mission area, avoiding obstacles like other flying cars, buildings, or terrain. 4. The mission ends when the player completes the mission objectives or fails due to time constraints or crashing. 5. The game saves the player’s progress, allowing them to continue to the next mission or retry the current one.

Alternative Flows: - If the player crashes during the mission, a crash animation is displayed, and they are given the option to retry or exit. - If the player selects a new car or upgrades their vehicle, the game updates the player’s vehicle before the next mission starts.

Special Requirements: - The game should feature dynamic environments with realistic driving and flying physics. - Multiplayer option could be implemented for players to race or compete with others in flying car missions.

Assumptions: - The game has been fully developed and has a stable build. - The player is familiar with basic flying car controls.
